Inclusivity Description

Just in time for Pride Month, Inclusivity has released Pride icons! Proceeds will go to LGBTQIA+ homeless youth centers.

Pride Icons

The Rainbow Flag

The most common symbol for the LGBTQIA+ community's pride and social movements. A flag was chosen to represent the community because flags are about proclaiming power.

One Love

This symbol represents the famous line of the community: Love is love. Every love is still love no matter the gender or sexual orientation.

The Rainbow

The rainbow and its colors were chosen because it fits the diversity of the community. It is also natural and universal in any language, which is exactly what the community is fighting to be seen as.

Together

Two hands holding each other and is in the shape of a heart represent the solidarity among the community and even every person that is needed in order to have equality.

Love is Love

Another symbol of love is love. Another symbol of how the LGBTQIA+'s community is diverse and that diversity shouldn't invalidate them.

Marriage Equality

Same-sex marriage, for the longest time, has been a human right deprived from members of the community. It is slowly being recognized around the world but there are still a lot of countries that haven't.

For Us All

Love, no matter who gives and receives it, is love. Love is love is love is love.

The Gays

Intertwined male and female symbols are used to show straight relationships. The intertwining of two male symbols acknowledge that men-loving-men relationships are valid, too.

The Girls

Intertwined male and female symbols are used to show straight relationships. The intertwining of two female symbols acknowledge that women-loving-women relationships are valid, too.

The Fight

The LGBTQIA+ community still has a long way to go in fighting for equal rights. It is why Pride exists, or why the community continuously fight for, celebrate, and remember themselves in a world that far too often invalidates them.
